RIZAL DAY


Rizal Day is a national holiday in the Philippines observed on December 30th each year. It commemorates the life and martyrdom of Dr. José Rizal, a prominent Filipino nationalist, writer, and revolutionary hero.

José Rizal was a key figure in the Philippine Revolution against Spanish colonial rule. He used his writings and intellectual prowess to inspire Filipinos to seek independence and advocate for social reforms. Rizal's novels, such as "Noli Me Tangere" and "El Filibusterismo," exposed the injustices and abuses of the Spanish colonial government, sparking a sense of national consciousness and awakening among Filipinos.

Rizal Day is a time for Filipinos to honor José Rizal's contributions to the Philippine struggle for independence and to reflect on his ideals of patriotism, selflessness, and love of country. It is observed with various activities, including wreath-laying ceremonies at Rizal Park (formerly Luneta Park) in Manila, where Rizal was executed by firing squad in 1896.
