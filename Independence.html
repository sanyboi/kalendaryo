LABOR DAY


Independence Day in the Philippines, also known as "Araw ng Kalayaan," is a national holiday celebrated on June 12th each year. It commemorates the Philippines' declaration of independence from Spanish colonial rule on June 12, 1898. The declaration was made by General Emilio Aguinaldo, who proclaimed Philippine independence in Kawit, Cavite, following the Filipino victory in the Battle of Alapan.

During Independence Day celebrations, various activities take place across the country, including flag-raising ceremonies, parades, cultural performances, historical reenactments, and patriotic displays. These events serve to honor the sacrifices of Filipino heroes and martyrs who fought for independence and to reaffirm national pride and unity.
